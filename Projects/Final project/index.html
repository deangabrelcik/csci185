Top 8 Mountain Ranges in the United States

  • 1. Rocky Mountains
  • 2. Sierra Nevada
  • 3. Cascade Range
  • 4. Coast Range
  • 5. Alaska Range
  • 6. Brooks Range
  • 7. Olympic Mountains
  • 8. Uinta Mountains

1. Rocky Mountains

The Rocky Mountains, commonly known as the Rockies, are a major mountain range in western North America.

2. Sierra Nevada

The Sierra Nevada is a mountain range in the Western United States, between the Central Valley of California and the Great Basin.

3. Cascade Range

The Cascade Range or Cascades is a major mountain range of western North America, extending from southern British Columbia through Washington and Oregon to Northern California.

4. Coast Range

The Pacific Coast Ranges, are the series of mountain ranges that stretch along the West Coast of North America from Alaska south to Northern and Central Mexico.

5. Alaska Range

The Alaska Range is a relatively narrow, 400-mile-long mountain range in the Southcentral region of the U.S. state of Alaska.

6. Brooks Range

The Brooks Range is a mountain range in far northern North America stretching some 700 miles from west to east across northern Alaska into Canada's Yukon Territory.

7. Olympic Mountains

The Olympic Mountains are a mountain range on the Olympic Peninsula of western Washington in the United States.

8. Uinta Mountains

The Uinta Mountains are an east-west trending chain of mountains in northeastern Utah extending slightly into southern Wyoming in the United States.
